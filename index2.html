THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN PADDINGS, MARGINS, AND BORDERS

Paddings, margins, and borders provide great ways to lay out a website in an organised and neat way. However, there are differences among the three.

A margin is the space around an element's border , while padding is the space between an element's border and its contents. In other words, margin controls the space outside an element, and padding controls the space inside an element. Borders simply wrap the element.

A border can wrap immediately around an element, or it may look further away from an element if more padding has been applied to the element. Margin is the space between the border and the next element of the design, in all directions.
